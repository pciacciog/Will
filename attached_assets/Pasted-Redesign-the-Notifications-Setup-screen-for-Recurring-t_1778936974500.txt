Redesign the Notifications Setup screen for Recurring type Wills.

---

SECTION 1: Track on

Section label: "TRACK ON" in small uppercase muted style

A single card containing seven day pills in a row: M T W T F S S
- Each pill is a circle, tappable to toggle on/off
- Selected (on): green filled circle, white text
- Deselected (off): light gray circle, muted text
- Default: all 7 days selected
- Minimum 1 day must remain selected at all times —
  prevent deselecting the last active day

Save the user's selected days to the Will record in the database
as an array (e.g. [0,1,2,3,4] for Mon–Fri, 0=Monday, 6=Sunday).

---

SECTION 2: Notifications

Section label: "NOTIFICATIONS" in small uppercase muted style

A single card with two slim rows:

Row 1 — Reminder
- Label: "Reminder" with subtle subtext "Before the moment"
- Tappable time in green (opens native iOS time picker)
- Toggle on/off — when off, time disappears
- Default: on, 8:30 PM

Row 2 — Check-in
- Label: "Check-in" with subtle subtext "How did it go?"
- Tappable time in green (opens native iOS time picker)
- Toggle on/off — when off, time disappears
- Default: on, 8:00 AM next morning

Subtext must always be generic — never say "tonight" or "this 
morning" since commitment time varies per Will.

---

NOTIFICATION FIRING RULES (critical):

Both the Reminder and Check-in notifications must ONLY fire on
days the user has selected in the "Track on" section.

Implementation:
- When scheduling notifications, iterate over the user's saved
  tracked days array
- Only schedule Reminder and Check-in pushes for those specific
  weekdays — skip all others entirely
- If the Will spans multiple weeks, repeat this pattern each week
  for the duration of the Will
- When the user updates their tracked days after creation (via
  Manage Will), cancel all existing scheduled notifications for
  this Will and reschedule based on the updated days array

---

TRACKING LOGIC:

Only tracked days count toward progress and can be marked as
missed. Untracked days must:
- Not appear as red (missed) in the calendar view
- Not be counted in the missed stat
- Not affect the success rate
- Be visually muted in the calendar — same treatment as
  pre-start days (grayed out, non-tappable)
